Methodology

Our analysis employs a mixed-methods approach combining:

  • Time Series Analysis - ARIMA and GARCH models to analyze historical exchange rate patterns
  • Regression Analysis - Multiple regression to identify key drivers of exchange rate volatility
  • Event Study Methodology - To isolate effects of policy changes and external shocks
  • Scenario Planning - For forecasting future exchange rate trajectories
  • Stakeholder Interviews - With economists, policymakers, and industry experts

Recommendations for Government

Based on our comprehensive exchange rate analysis, we recommend the following fiscal, structural, and trade policies for the Egyptian government to support exchange rate stability and sustainable economic growth.

Fiscal Policy Reforms:

  1. Debt Sustainability

    Implement a clear medium-term debt reduction plan targeting a debt-to-GDP ratio below 80% by 2028 and 70% by 2030. Extend debt maturities and increase the share of domestic currency debt to reduce FX risks.

  2. Revenue Enhancement

    Broaden the tax base through improved compliance and reduced exemptions rather than rate increases. Target an increase in tax-to-GDP ratio from current 14% to at least 18% by 2028.

  3. Expenditure Rationalization

    Continue subsidy reform while strengthening targeted social safety nets. Shift spending from subsidies to productive investments in infrastructure, education, and healthcare.

Structural Reforms:

  1. Business Environment Enhancement

    Streamline licensing procedures, reduce bureaucratic barriers, and enforce contracts more effectively. Target improving Egypt's Ease of Doing Business ranking to top 50 globally by 2028 (currently 114th).

  2. SOE Reform

    Implement comprehensive state-owned enterprise reform to enhance efficiency, improve governance, and reduce fiscal drain. Develop clear privatization roadmap for non-strategic enterprises.

  3. Financial Inclusion

    Accelerate digital financial services adoption targeting 70% financial inclusion by 2027 (currently 38%). Enhance SME access to finance through credit guarantee schemes and tailored financial products.

Trade & Investment Policy:

  1. Export Promotion Strategy

    Develop comprehensive export strategy targeting high-value-added sectors including pharmaceuticals, electronics, agri-business, and IT services. Target doubling non-petroleum exports by 2030.

  2. Investment Promotion

    Establish specialized investment promotion units for key sectors with clear performance metrics. Develop specialized economic zones with enhanced infrastructure and streamlined regulations.

  3. Africa Integration

    Leverage the African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA) by developing logistics hubs and trade facilitation mechanisms making Egypt a gateway to Africa for international businesses.

Social Protection Enhancement:

  • Expand Takaful & Karama cash transfer programs to cover 4-5 million households
  • Develop adaptive social protection mechanisms that automatically expand during economic shocks
  • Implement food security programs to reduce vulnerability to international price fluctuations
  • Enhance skills development programs to improve labor market outcomes
  • Develop housing solutions for lower and middle-income segments

Key Devaluation Events

2016

November 2016 Devaluation

The Central Bank of Egypt floated the Egyptian pound, resulting in an immediate 32.3% devaluation.

2022

March 2022 Devaluation

Egypt devalued its currency by approximately 14% in response to the economic impacts of the Russia-Ukraine conflict.

2024

March 2024 Devaluation

The Egyptian pound was devalued by 38% to unify exchange rates, supported by a 600 basis point interest rate hike.

Impact on Inflation & Purchasing Power

The purchasing power of 1,000 EGP in 2011 is equivalent to approximately 135 EGP in 2025, representing an 86.5% decrease in purchasing power over the 14-year period.
